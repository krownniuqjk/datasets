# Notes:
 Prenasalised consonants in the paper are represented by a superscript nasal followed by the oral stop.
 It is not possible for me to recreate this other than the alveolar nasal.
 The alveolar nasal is marked long when it is a distinct phoneme and precedes 
 the affricate /dʒ/, in contrast to the prenasalised affricate /ndʒ/.
 The author does not mention short [ŏ] in the paper.  It has been added as an allophone of /o/.
